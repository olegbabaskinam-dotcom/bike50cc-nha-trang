How to choose your first 50cc bike in Nha Trang

If you are buying a bike in Vietnam for the first time, it's overwhelming: hundreds of listings, different models, unclear prices and the constant fear of getting junk. This guide sorts it out — so you pick a reliable 50cc bike, licence-free, and just ride.

Automatic or manual — what's easier

Automatic (xe ga) — twist the throttle and go, no gears. Perfect if you haven't ridden before or are here short-term.

Manual (Honda Wave, Cub) — more reliable, cheaper to fix, a nearly indestructible engine, but you must shift gears. Great if you ride confidently and stay long.

For most tourists and beginners we recommend automatic.

What to check when buying

  • Documents (cà vẹt). In hand, clean, and stating 50cc — otherwise "no licence" doesn't apply.
  • Mileage. Under 20–30k km is great. 50k+ is risky. 90k — parts only.
  • Engine and frame number. Must match the documents. A mismatch ("sai số máy") is a red flag.
  • Condition. Start it cold, check lights, brakes, engine noise, plastic.

Which models to buy

  • Honda Vision / Honda Scoopy — reliable automatics, economical and cute. A great first bike.
  • Vespa-style (Nioshima, Elegant) — stylish, photogenic, hold value.
  • Honda Cub / retro-cúp — vintage classic, an eternal engine.
  • Honda Wave — the most reliable and cheapest to run, manual.
