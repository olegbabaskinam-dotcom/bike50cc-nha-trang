Licence & police

Do you need a licence for a 50cc bike in Vietnam?

Short answer — no. In Vietnam, motorbikes with an engine up to 50cc fall into a category that does not require a driving licence. That's exactly why 50cc bikes are so popular with tourists and long-stayers: hop on and go, no licence paperwork.

What about the police?

Traffic police (CSGT) do not fine you for riding a 50cc bike without a licence — it's legal. But some things are checked for everyone, regardless of engine size:

  • Helmet — required for rider and passenger. The most common reason for a fine.
  • Bike documents (registration, cà vẹt) — must be valid and with you.
  • Sobriety — drink-driving is fined strictly; engine size is irrelevant here.

Why this matters when buying

"50cc, no licence" only works if the bike is genuinely 50cc on the documents and the papers are clean. If the papers say a larger engine or the bike has a shady history, the benefit disappears. That's why we sell only bikes checked against the police database, with a full set of documents.

In short

  • 50cc — no licence, legal.
  • Helmet — always required.
  • Bike documents — clean and with you.
  • Buy from someone who guarantees clean papers.
